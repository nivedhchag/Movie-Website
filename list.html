Schindler's List

Schindler's List is a 1993 historical drama film about Oskar Schindler, a German industrialist who saved more than 1,100 Jewish refugees from the Holocaust during World War II: Plot The film follows Schindler's transformation from a war profiteer to a humanitarian who employs Jewish workers in his factories to protect them from the SS. It also shows the struggles of those who survived the Holocaust because of Schindler's actions. Cast Liam Neeson plays Schindler, Ralph Fiennes plays SS officer Amon Göth, and Ben Kingsley plays Schindler's Jewish accountant Itzhak Stern. Awards The film won seven Academy Awards, including Best Picture and Best Director, and was nominated for twelve. It also won seven BAFTAs and three Golden Globe Awards. Rating Schindler's List is rated R for language, sexuality, and violence. Common Sense Media rates it as appropriate for ages 15+. Where to watch You can watch Schindler's List on Amazon Prime and other streaming platforms, or buy the DVD or Blu-ray.
