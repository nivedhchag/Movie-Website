Mughal-e-Azam

Mughal-e-Azam is a 1960 Indian historical drama film about the love affair between a Mughal prince and a court dancer: Plot The film is set in the 16th century and tells the story of the doomed romance between Mughal Prince Salim and Anarkali, a court dancer. The emperor, Salim's father, disapproves of the relationship and imprisons Anarkali, which leads to a war between father and son. Cast The film stars Prithviraj Kapoor as Emperor Akbar, Dilip Kumar as Prince Salim, Madhubala as Anarkali, and Durga Khote as Queen Jodha. Production The film was directed by K. Asif and written by Aman, Kamal Amrohi, K. Asif, Wajahat Mirza, and Ehsan Rizvi. It was based on the 1922 play Anarkali by Imtiaz Ali Taj. Known for The film is known for its Mughal grandeur, royal traditions, and the mythical character of Anarkali. It also features a battle scene that set a new standard for military spectacle and pyrotechnic effects. Trivia It took 16 years to complete the film. It was also the first full-length movie to be revived and colorized for a theatrical re-release.
